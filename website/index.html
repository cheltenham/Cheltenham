What can you say about the place we call home?


Four primary builders broke ground here in 1989. By 1993, a total of 157 homes on 158 lots became "Cheltenham."

Located in historic Centerville, Ohio, our residents have nearby access to parks, churches, and some of the best schools in the area. We are bounded to the south by Alex-Bell Road, to the east by Wilmington Pike, and to the west by the Nestle Creek sub-division and Greene Line Park.

Cheltenham residents come from all walks of life, most are professionals. A few of our residents call Cheltenham their retirement home, many built or moved here because it's a great place for children and the young of heart.

We typically meet the 4th Monday evening of every month at different homes. We select books by suggestions offered. When the book club gets together, we usually discuss titles and reviews of books that look interesting. Then we, as a group, make some selections of what we will read. We try to plan at least two months in advance so the faster readers can read ahead, and the slower readers have time to read the books. Oftentimes our significant others call the club the wine club, but honestly we do discuss the books in detail. We read both fiction and nonfiction titles, so the club gives readers the opportunity to read genres that they normally would not read. Cheltenham Covenants

Below is a summary of the salient points of our Cheltenham convenants for your reference. All homeowners need to follow these covenants in order to preserve the attractive appearance for which our Cheltenham neighborhood has become known.


Please refer to the published Cheltenham Covenants for a complete listing.


  • Maintain mailbox in good repair
  • Grass cut regularly
  • Sidewalks cleared of grass clippings in summer & snow in winter
  • Landscaping free of weeds
  • Front light turned on after dark
  • No parked cars in street overnight
  • No chain link fences
  • No utility sheds constructed away from houses
  • No above ground pools
  • All mailboxes are to be wood clad
  • Seek approval for architectural or external color changes from the review committee

Also, please observe the 25-mph speed limit and help keep our neighborhood safe by reporting any suspicious activity to the Centerville Police Department.
